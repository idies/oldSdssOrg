SDSS Data Access by Graduate Students and Postdocs

April 1999

  1. A graduate student at a Sloan Institution may have access to SDSS data as long as he is a graduate student at a Sloan Institution and provided that a Participant at their institution sponsors him. The Sponsor shall be responsible for the student using the SDSS data responsibly and abiding by all Collaboration rules. The Sponsor shall inform the Spokesperson of his sponsorship of the student, the termination of the sponsorship when the student leaves or graduates, and the title and place of publication of the student's thesis if it involves SDSS data. The Sponsor may terminate his sponsorship at any time by informing the Spokesperson.

  2. A graduate student at a non-Sloan institution working with a Sloan Participant may obtain access to SDSS data to work on a specific project with his Participant Ph.D. adviser by requesting permission to do so from the Collaboration Council. The Collaboration Council will judge such requests by the same standards they use for requests for any non-Participant to work on a specific project and shall make a recommendation to the Management Council. The Management Council will make the final decision and communicate to the student. If granted, data access shall be restricted to the specific student for the specific project. The Participant sponsor shall inform the Spokesperson of the title and place of publication of the student's Ph.D. thesis.

  3. A postdoc at a Sloan Institution who does not have PI status, or is not employed full time to work on the SDSS, may have access to SDSS data if more than 50% of his salary is paid by the Institution and if a Participant at that Institution agrees to sponsor him (see item 1 for the obligations of sponsorship).
