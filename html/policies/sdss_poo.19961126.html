Introductory Background

The initial Sloan Digital Sky Survey (SDSS) Principles of Operation (PoO) were drafted during 1989 and approved by the Astrophysical Research Consortium (ARC) Board of Governors in February 1990. The document served the intended purpose very well for a few years. However, the passage of time brought circumstances and events which were not anticipated in the 1990 document. Notable among these was the addition of four more Participating Institutions: Johns Hopkins University and University of Washington joined the original ARC members University of Chicago, Princeton University, and the Institute for Advanced Study; the Japan Promotional Group and the US Naval Observatory joined Fermilab as non-ARC participants under Memoranda of Understanding. By 1995, there was consensus among ARC Board members and representatives of SDSS Participating Institutions that the PoO should be updated in combination with a change in the model through which the ARC Board managed the SDSS project.

Accordingly, at its November 20, 1995 meeting, the ARC Board adopted a resolution to dissolve the SDSS Subcommittee of the Board as of December 31, 1995 and establish in its place, effective January 1, 1996, a new SDSS Advisory Council. The resolution charged the SDSS Director and new Advisory Council to review and update the PoO in 1996 for approval by the Board. This is the updated PoO approved by the ARC Board (November 26, 1996). This document supersedes the 1990 document, but does not invalidate any actions taken under the 1990 document.

I) PREAMBLE

  1. The goal of the Sloan Digital Sky Survey (SDSS) is to build and operate a dedicated telescope suitable for wide-angle surveys of the sky which will address critical issues in extragalactic astronomy, especially in the field of large-scale structure. Specifically, a digitized multiband photometric survey at high northern galactic latitudes (b > 30 degrees) will be conducted, and in the same area a multifiber spectroscopic survey of galaxies and quasars will be performed. This will be supplemented by a deeper survey with more restricted sky coverage in the southern galactic hemisphere. Finally, as time and resources allow, the photometric survey will be extended to cover the remainder of the available sky. The photometric survey will serve both to select the galaxies and quasars for the spectroscopic survey, and as an archival data base in its own right. The spectroscopic survey will be primarily designed to yield accurate redshifts. The primary survey is expected to take five years to complete, after a suitable commissioning phase.

  2. The top priority will be to ensure that the scientific integrity of the primary surveys is the highest possible: calibrations must be extensive and trustworthy. Moreover, the intent is that the survey be well enough designed and the data of sufficient quality so that it will have a long and productive lifetime.

  3. There will be two main products of this survey which constitute the core of the project: the Archive, a reliable and easily utilized data base which will be made available to the public, and the Scientific Analyses of the data in the Archive which will provide insight into the nature and origin of large-scale structure and achieve other important scientific goals.

    Using the Archive constructed by the SDSS project, and provided to the Participants, the Participants may carry out whatever scientific projects they choose, subject to the policy guidelines discussed in Section IV and Section V. They must provide their own funding for needed resources, above and beyond the Archive and the software tools provided by the project as part of the Archive.

    Consistent with plans to maintain the integrity and usability of the Archive, the Participating Institutions intend to release the first two years of survey data at a time prior to final completion of the survey, in order to provide a "preview" of the full survey.

    Work aimed at extracting intermediate scientific results as the survey progresses will be encouraged. However, Participants engaged in this work must provide their own extra resources as needed and help in the overall quality control effort (indeed, such work is an essential part of this effort).

    The intent is to make data public within two years of the completion of the survey. Details of the data and publication policy for Participants and their collaborators are presented in Section IV.

  4. In addition to the primary surveys and the photometric survey of the available Galactic disk, Initiatives for other, separately funded projects to use available telescope time or the Archive in new ways will be entertained provided that they do not interfere with or significantly delay the primary surveys. These may include spectroscopic work in the Galactic plane or analyses of archived data which are scientifically distinct from the core program.

  5. This document presents the Principles of Operation (PoO) of the Sloan Digital Sky Survey, and they have been agreed upon by the Participating Institutions. They may be revised as needed by future agreements.

II) MANAGEMENT

  1. Board

    2. Ultimate responsibility for all aspects of the SDSS project, including approval of annual budgets and requests for outside funding for the SDSS project, rests with the Board. The Board is assisted by the Advisory Council on matters concerning the SDSS project. The Board must approve entry of all Participating Institutions into the SDSS project; one criterion for entry will be the written acceptance of these PoO. Changes to the PoO must be approved by the Board and will generally be based on recommendations from the Advisory Council, acting either at its own initiative or in response to a request from the Board.

  2. Advisory Council

    3. In addition to advising the Board on matters relating to the SDSS project, members of the Advisory Council represent the Participating Institutions and are therefore the primary channel through which information flows between the Board on the one hand, and the Participants and Participating Institutions on the other.

    The Advisory Council will be appointed by the Board from among nominations by the Participating Institutions. Each Participating Institution will be represented by at least one and not more than two members. The Chair of the Board will be an ex officio member of the Advisory Council. The Chair of the Advisory Council will be appointed by the Board for a specified term.

    The Advisory Council will meet as deemed necessary, but generally at least three times per year. Meetings may be conducted in-person or via teleconferencing. Meeting dates will be set and announced well in advance to assure good attendance. The agenda will be distributed at least five (5) days in advance of the meeting to Advisory Council members plus the Chair and Secretary of the Board, and the Chair of the SAC. The Advisory Council Chair is responsible for timely filing of approved meeting minutes with the ARC Secretary.

    The Advisory Council will identify and evaluate candidates for the position of Director when the incumbent's term is coming to an end, or when the position is vacated unexpectedly. The Director will be appointed by the Board taking into consideration the recommendation of the Advisory Council.

  3. Director

    4. The Board has delegated to the Director the executive authority for construction of the telescope and related hardware and software, and for operation of the survey. To those ends, the Director is responsible for:

    • selecting key personnel;
    • keeping the Advisory Council informed on the status of the project, including any major problems and plans for resolving them;
    • working with ARC officers in conducting the project so as to comply with the terms of funding awards received by ARC from federal agencies and private entities;
    • drafting for concurrence by the Advisory Council and approval by the Board the Memorandum of Understanding with any non-ARC member Participating Institution.

    The Director will submit annual budgets to the Advisory Council. These budgets will include funds and in-kind services needed for planned construction, maintenance, and use of the SDSS telescope, instruments, and Archive. The Advisory Council will transmit the Director's budget along with its recommendations to the Board for approval.

    The Director will provide financial summaries to the Advisory Council monthly to show expenditures and obligations compared to the major line item budget. The Director will be assisted in preparing annual budgets and monthly financial summaries by the ARC corporate office under the general supervision of the ARC Treasurer.

  4. Science Advisory Committee (SAC)

    5. The Director appoints the Science Advisory Committee (SAC) Chair for a fixed term after consultation with representatives of the Participating Institutions. Other members of the SAC are appointed by the Director for a fixed term in consultation with the SAC Chair. These SAC members include one representative from each of the Working Groups, plus others at the Director's discretion. In consultation with the Director, the SAC Chair may appoint ad hoc groups to assist with specific science issues or scientific tasks.

    The SAC's advisory responsibility embraces the broad spectrum of science issues inherent in successful implementation and conduct of the SDSS project. These include, but are not limited to, policies concerning instrumentation, survey strategy, telescope scheduling, access to science data, and publication. The SAC reviews Proposals and Initiatives and reports their recommendations to the Director. These responsibilities are described more fully in Section V.

    Informal communication among members of the SAC, Board, and Advisory Council is encouraged. Any formal Board or Advisory Council communications with the SAC is through the Director. These communications may include inviting the SAC Chair and/or other SAC members to participate in meetings of the Board or the Advisory Council.

  5. Working Groups (WG)

    6. WGs of the SDSS project have been formed to address the following specific topics: large scale structure, clusters, galaxies, quasars, stars, serendipity, southern survey, reddening, and solar system. The function of these groups is to consider the complete flow of the data relevant to each WG from the CCD's through to the final publication of data and analysis. The WGs will define requirements for equipment, observing time, analysis software, and archiving. These specifications will be considered by the SAC in formulating recommendations to the Director in the same categories.

    A representative (who may or may not be the Chair) of each WG will be appointed to the SAC by the Director in consultation with the SAC Chair. The Director appoints Chairs of existing WGs. The appointment of each WG Chair will be for a fixed term.

    New WGs may be formed and existing WGs may be disbanded by the submission of a written plan to the SAC Chair. The SAC must concur before further action is taken. Where the plan calls for a new WG, it may be implemented when the SAC has concurred and the Advisory Council has approved the Director's recommendation for the WG Chair. Where the plan calls for disbanding an existing WG, concurrence by the SAC is the final action.

    It is imperative that the Advisory Council be fully informed on SDSS science issues. Accordingly, Advisory Council meeting agendas will frequently include reports on these issues by the Director and the SAC Chair.

III) SCIENTIFIC GOALS

  1. Primary Surveys

    1. In the pi steradians of the North Galactic Pole region (defined approximately by b>30 deg) we shall conduct surveys in the following manner:

      1. Obtain a photometric survey in five filters to 23rd magnitude (r', five sigma) for stellar objects;
      2. Obtain redshifts for all galaxies brighter than r'=18.
      3. Obtain redshifts for all quasars brighter than r'=19 identifiable by photometric indices.
      4. Obtain spectra of a limited number of other targets of high scientific importance, as defined by the SAC.

      The velocity precision for galaxies should in all cases be limited by photon noise and should be better than 60 km/s for normal galaxies at the survey limit. The data should be archived as a full data set of all pixels on the sky as well as in reduced data sets for later analysis and distribution. Such reduced data sets include atlas images of all detected objects, catalogs of image parameters for all detected objects, and one-dimensional spectra and catalogs of redshifts and spectral properties of all objects for which spectra have been obtained.

    2. In the available South Galactic Pole region there will be a deeper survey in a strip 2.5 degrees wide and approximately 90 degrees long, in order to:

      1. Obtain a photometric survey in five filters to about 25th magnitude (r', 5 sigma) for stellar objects by coaddition of repeated images;
      2. Investigate motion and variability in that region over the duration of the survey, as derived from the scans obtained for A.ii.a above;
      3. Obtain spectra for all objects to suitably defined limits somewhat fainter than defined in i.b and i.c above in some substantial fraction of the survey area, and
      4. Conduct other spectroscopic investigations as are deemed appropriate to explore the spectroscopic selection criteria used in the Northern Survey.

    3. In the additional available sky covering the Galactic disk, a deep photometric survey will be conducted on a best-effort basis to obtain a deep imaging survey to the same sensitivity as the Northern survey.

    Prioritization of resources - financial, human, and telescope availability - is indicated by the ordering (i), (ii), (iii) above. The jointly raised funds will be dedicated to these projects. Completion of project (iii) may require additional time and funding.

  2. Additional Projects

    1. Spectroscopic study of objects in the Galactic disk or of objects in the North Galactic Pole region not studied in (A.i.b), (A.i.c) or (A.i.d) above, performed on a basis which is strictly non-interfering with the primary surveys.
    2. Analysis of archived data from (A.i), (A.ii), or (A.iii) above for scientific purposes distinct from the core scientific program (A) outlined above (cf. Section I.D).

IV) DATA AND PUBLICATION POLICIES

  1. Participants

    1. Participants have access to all the primary and reduced data from the primary surveys ("core survey data"). Participants must read and abide by the PoO, and are responsible for protecting the scientific integrity of the SDSS and the data rights of other Participants.

      At the ARC Participating Institutions, Participants are defined to be the full-time PhD-level scientific staff (receive at least 50% of their annual salary from that institution). They must also either have Principal Investigator status at that institution, or be primarily employed to work on the SDSS, or have performed the equivalent of two years service to the SDSS (as judged by the SAC).

      Participants at the other Participating Institutions are defined via the individual MOU's.

      The SAC may also designate other scientists as Participants.

    2. It is anticipated that the scientific results of the core surveys will be published primarily and jointly by the Participants belonging to the WGs, with other Participants added by mutual agreement.

  2. Projects and Publications

    1. Participants must notify each other on initiation of any scientific project utilizing primary survey data that is expected ultimately to result in a publication. Electronic mail is suitable, and the SAC will maintain an accessible file ("project list"), which lists, for each project, the subject matter, known collaborators, a contact person (with address), and the desired date for completion of the project and submission of a manuscript for publication.

    2. At least one month before submission of any manuscript for publication which utilizes core survey data, the submission plan must be publicized to all Participants by electronic mail, with paper title, authors, and journal of submission added to an accessible file, kept by the SAC, entitled the "publication list". By "publication", we signify submission either to a scientific journal or to any other public forum.

  3. Authorship

    1. Any Participant, at any stage, of a research project may request that his/her name be added to the list of authors, with the presumption that permission will be granted if there has been any significant contribution to the project or to the SDSS infrastructure.

    2. If there are disputes among Participants on authorship, the SAC will recommend a solution of these to the Director, who will rule on individual cases. In the unlikely event of protest of such rulings, the issue may be appealed to the Advisory Council. It will have jurisdiction on the basis of a majority vote.

  4. Protection of Survey Scientific Integrity

    1. If any project is added to the publication list without due notice on the project list, the Director may rule for a delay in publication on the advice of the SAC with the same rights of appeal (cf. IV C.2).

    2. If any project is added to the publication list which the Director, on the basis of advice from the SAC, deems not technically ready for publication, the Director may rule for delay, with the same right of appeal.

    3. Any Participant who submits for publication results based on core survey data without notifying colleague Participants by listing the project in the publication list and any Participant violating rulings of the Director on the above noted matters, may be barred from further access to the Survey data by the Director, on advice of the SAC, with the same right of appeal.

  5. Further Guidelines

    6. The SAC may develope more detailed guidelines concerning the above, as they deem appropriate. A copy of these guidelines would be available from the Chair of the SAC, and should be read and agreed to by Participants and Participating Institutions.

V) PROPOSALS AND INITIATIVES

  1. Proposals to Construct or Operate the SDSS

    2. Proposals submitted by ARC as-a-whole to Federal or philanthropic sources to secure the funds to support the construction and operation of the SDSS require approval by the ARC Board of Governors following a recommendation by the Advisory Council. Such Proposals take precedence over other SDSS-related Proposals by Participants, and the Board may take appropriate steps to protect them, with proper notification to the Participants.

  2. Proposals to Fund Scientific Research

    3. During the period prior to the first significant public release of SDSS data, a set of policies, procedures, and guidelines will apply to applications by Participants that seek substantial (more than $30K total) funding from US Federal sources for the primary purpose of supporting research using SDSS data. The purposes of such strictures are to protect the scientific integrity of the SDSS; to foster equity and collegiality within the collaboration; to present an organized, rational funding plan to financial sponsors and the scientific community; to insure that the SDSS data are described in a technically-responsible manner; and to insure that the Proposals do not contain items or describe arrangements that conflict with the PoO, the MOUs, or the agreements already in place with major SDSS funding sources.

    Except as specifically noted below, approaches to philanthropic or state sources are not subject to these strictures. Participating Institutions should keep one another informed about such activity on a best-effort basis through their members on the Advisory Council and ARC Board.

    Proposals will fall into two categories: Key Projects and everything else.

    1. It is the responsibility of each Working Group (WG) to define a set of Key Projects that constitute the core science of that WG.

    2. The WGs will determine the science team members and leaders for these projects.

    3. The specific criteria and procedures used by a WG to select Key Projects, team members, and leaders will be developed by that WG. However, these criteria should be based primarily on maximizing the scientific return. Due recognition should also be given to those who have made major contributions to the SDSS, and the balance between the junior and senior members of the WG should be equitably reflected.

    4. The SAC will collect and organize these projects. The SAC Chair in consultation with SAC members will insure that the guidelines described above have been followed and will adjudicate disputes. If the SAC judges that a WG has not developed an adequate set of Key Projects, the SAC may augment the list of projects and solicit interest from the Participants in the formation of the additional teams.

    5. The SAC Chair will forward the recommended set of Key Project abstracts, team members, and team leaders to the Director for approval. The Director will address any institutional issues that may arise, such as adequate representation of the ARC and MOU Participating Institutions.

    6. These Key Project teams will respond to funding opportunities by developing and submitting Proposals. The PI on such a Key Project Proposal need not be the team leader (e.g. as in the case of a team headed by a member of the JPG), but his/her institution will assume primary administrative responsibility. These proposals will be vetted by the SAC prior to submission, following which the Director may formally endorse such Proposals as representing the considered and coordinated effort of the SDSS team in that key area of research.

    7. It is the responsibility of a PI or team leader to inform the relevant WG Chair as to the status of their Proposal. In the event that a Proposal for a Key Project is declined by the funding agency, the WG may choose to significantly modify the scientific content and team membership (in a manner consistent with the above). Such changes would be reviewed by the SAC and their recommendations sent to the Director for approval.

    8. The intention to submit a Proposal (whether or not it is a Key Project) shall be announced by the PI to the Participants in the form of a publically-posted title, brief research summary, list of team members, and estimated total budget at least one month prior to Proposal submission. The SAC will maintain an accessible file ("proposal list") for this purpose. The project would appreciate a posting of the disposition of the Proposal when that is determined.

    9. Following the first public release of a significant amount of SDSS data (a milestone to be defined by the SAC), the above policies and procedures become null and void.

    10. Proposing institutions are responsible for all incremental costs deriving from the proposed research.

    11. All Proposals by individuals or teams for support for SDSS science (whether or not they fall in the realm of Key Projects, and whether they are to federal, state, or philanthropic sources) shall be carefully vetted by the PI's institution to insure that they do not contain items or describe arrangements that conflict with the PoO, the MOUs, or the agreements already in place with major SDSS funding sources. The Director will develop a summary of such sensitive issues and distribute it to the relevant parties at the Participating Institutions. More generally, it is the responsibility of the members of the Advisory Council to insure that Participants and the relevant institutional officers (e.g. Departmental Chair, Director of Sponsored Programs, etc.) are informed of the above procedures, as is implied by that institution's acceptance of the PoO.

    12. Persons blatantly violating these policies may have their status as a Participant revoked on the basis of a majority vote by the Advisory Council following the recommendation of the Director.

  3. Extension Beyond the Core Program

    4. Initiatives to use or augment the SDSS hardware, and/or data archive, and/or operations for purposes that are not part of the core program will be reviewed by the SAC on the basis of their scientific merit and by the Director to insure their technical appropriateness and assess their financial impact. The proponents will be responsible for any resulting incremental costs.

    The Director will prepare a recommendation to the Advisory Council, which will determine the extent of any required modification of the PoO. The Chair of the Advisory Council will transmit the Initiative, the Director's recommendation, and the Advisory Council's own recommendations to the Board. Approval of the Initiative may be granted contingent upon the proponents securing the necessary financial resources.

  4. Early Collaborative Use of the Data Archive by Non-Participants

    5. Prior to the public release of the data archive or the publication of the relevant portions thereof, non-Participants may request to conduct research with, or otherwise make use of, limited portions of the SDSS data archive in collaboration with Participants. Such Initiatives may involve the provision of support, resources, observational or computational facilities, or expertise by the collaborating non-Participants. The proponents will be entirely responsible for any associated incremental costs. The collaborating Participants will be responsible for protecting the scientific integrity of the SDSS and the data rights of other Participants.

    These Initiatives will be reviewed by the SAC and then by the Director. A subsequent review by the Advisory Council and approval by the Board is required if the Director finds the Initiative meritorious, but determines that there may be a financial impact on the Participating Institutions or a potential conflict with the existing institutional arrangements.

  5. Use of Intellectual Property

    6. Initiatives to use the intellectual property of the SDSS collaboration will be covered by the Intellectual Property Policy of ARC, Inc., a copy of which is available from the ARC Corporate Secretary.

VI) RELATIONSHIP TO NON-PARTICIPATING ARC INSTITUTIONS

The SDSS was undertaken on the premise that all ARC scientists would enjoy direct and indirect benefits from it. Major scientific and financial benefits include:

  1. All ARC scientists may submit Initiatives to the SAC involving either limited early access to portions of the Archive (Section V.D), or the extension of the SDSS beyond the core surveys (Section V.C). Evaluation will be based primarily on scientific merit and technical appropriateness, and secondarily on institutional affiliation. One member from a non-Participating ARC institution will be invited by the Director to be present when the SAC evaluates such Initiatives.

  2. The SDSS facilities will operate on ARC's Apache Point site and share operations staff with the 3.5m telescope, affording both projects the benefits of a broader skill mix. Staff and other common operations support costs will be apportioned between the projects by mutual agreement of the directors.

  3. Results from SDSS site monitoring equipment will be made available to users of the 3.5m telescope.

  4. The SDSS project has repaid the 3.5m telescope project a prorata share of the initial site infrastructure costs, under a rationale approved by the Board.

  5. When the SDSS project is completed, operation and/or disposition of SDSS facilities will be determined by the Board.
