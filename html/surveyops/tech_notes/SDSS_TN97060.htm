Instrument Lift

Sloan Digital Sky Survey Telescope Technical Note TN97060
 
Carl Lindenmeyer October 23, 1997

On Oct. 12, 1997 the SDSS instrument lift was tested with a 1400 Lb. load. The system was cycled many times and no problems were observed. The reported "vibration" was caused by a dry piston rod on the hydraulic cylinder, oiling the rod lightly with oil solved the problem.

The system pressure is set at 760 PSI at the pump with the relief valve setting slightly higher to avoid "teasing" the relief valve with minor pressure changes. Only one pressure is used for both directions and for all instruments lifted.

Except for some Questions about the electrical connection to the MCP, the lift is ready for use. Dan Long and Steve Bastian were shown how to operate the system using the manual control.
