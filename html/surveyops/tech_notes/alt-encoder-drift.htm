Measured Drift in the 2.5-m Telescope Altitude Drive Encoders

French Leger, Peregrine McGehee, and Bill Boroski
March 1, 2000

Sloan Digital Sky Survey Technical Note TN-2000-010

A simple test was conducted on 2/24/00 to quantify the drift over time in the altitude drive encoders.  The test was conducted by turning off the drive motors, applying the telescope altitude brake, and logging encoder readings in the Telescope Performance Monitor (TPM).   The test was done overnight with no one around the telescope.  Average oustide temperature during the measurement period was approximately -7°C.

Below are the min and max altitude encoder readings for blocks of 10 minutes. Over ~2 hours there is a systematic increase of 10 counts (~0.14 arcsec).  The full data set are contained in the following TPM logfile on sdsshost:  /mcptpm/brakes.dat
