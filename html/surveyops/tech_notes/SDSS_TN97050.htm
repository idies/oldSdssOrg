Cartridge Installation & Removal Clearance
Between Slit Head and Spectroscope Door

Sloan Digital Sky Survey Telescope Technical Note TN97050
 
Carl Lindenmeyer October 23, 1997

During instrument exchange operations, errors in telescope altitude and rotator position translate into essentially East-West errors in the position of the opening in the spectrograph which must accept the fiber cartridge slit head.

The original specifications called for a +/-0.2o tolerance in the altitude and rotator positions. This was changed to a tolerance of +/- 0.05o for altitude axis position and +/-0.1o for rotator position. This change enabled coupling of the cartridge kinematic mounts but offers too little room for error in the slit head to spectroscope opening.

A change to +/-0.05o in rotator error is requested to permit safe instrument transfer. The attached scale drawing illustrates the effect of the various tolerances.

Keep in mind that the error in telescope position is effectively doubled because the cartridge could be at the opposite extreme position. The drawing shows the slit head at exactly the same error as the telescope which is impossible due to tolerances in the many components involved in the location of the cartridge. Therefore, less space will, in reality, be available than shown.
