Instrument Rotator Torque Measurements
Prior to Installing Guide Roller Shims

Sloan Digital Sky Survey Telescope Technical Note TN99027
 
French Leger, February 22, 1999

The following thickness and torque measurements were taken on the new instrument rotator on 2/22/99. Thickness measurements were taken with a micrometer. Torque measurements were made by pulling on a spring scale attached the instrument rotator at a position 54" from the axis of rotation. The rotator cable wrap ws not in place on the rotator during these measurements.

Torque readings were taken at 45° intervals and with break-away and running forces recorded. Telescope was set at 90° (bird bath) altitude without any weight mounted to it. Force was applied at 54" radius. Test was conducted for one complete rotation (360°) and then returned to 0° position in the opposite direction.

The running force was "jumpy" during the preload test.

The solution to the problem of the higher torque condition is to put a shim into each preload roller arm and set the belleville washers to the required load. This will maintain the proper preload on the guide rollers but will prevent the rollers from "pinching" the rotator rim. The torque measurements will be repeated after the shims have been installed.
