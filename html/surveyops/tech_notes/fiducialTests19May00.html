2.5-meter Telescope Altitude Fiducial Repeatability Tests (5/19/00)
 

A series of sweep tests were made on the altitude axis of the SDSS 2.5-meter telescope to quantify the repeatability of the altitude encoder/fiducial system.  The tests were conducted and data collected by Carlos Gonzalez using the following procedure:

  1. The telescope altitude axis was fiducialized following the standard procedure. This process initializes the encoder counts for the “15-degree” fiducial crossing point.

  2. The telescope was moved up and down in altitude a number of times. The data table below shows the range over which the altitude was moved, the number of sweeps made, and the velocity at which the telescope was moving.  Note that one sweep consists of one complete up and down movement.

  3. After the required number of sweeps, the telescope was moved to 14.5 degrees in altitude, then commanded to move to 16:00:00 at a velocity of 5000 counts per second (cps). In doing so, the telescope crossed the 15-degree fiducial.

  4. With the telescope sitting stable at 16:00:00, the altitude axis was re-fiducialized to re-initialize the encoder reading to the 15-degree fiducial crossing point.

  5. The change in encoder reading was noted by recording the new telescope position displayed on the MCP Menu display screen.
