TR97020
SDSS Trip Report
Telescope Motion and Lift Tests
July 23 to 27, 1997

Charlie Briegel
Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory
Batavia, IL   USA    60510

APO Work Summary

  1. Telescope motion tests
    1. Moved rotator with VME-PC via offset and PID
    2. We thought we had found some good coefficients for the PID loop, but could not reproduce good motion after breaking for a cup of coffee. It appeared as if the friction in the drive had changed.
    3. Drove rotator with VME-MCP via offset and PID, but PID oscillated. Smoked Sten's power amplifier before I could dampen the rotator oscillations with different coefficients. Smoked the amp by trying to turn it off with the electrical strip... cycled the power.
    4. Sten and Claudio made some good measurements on all three axes.


  2. Instrument Lift
    1. Tested motion similar to FNAL test with additions such as backoff from out of tolerance force.
    2. Noticed VME crate power off causes all outputs to go active... result of active low logic.
    3. Ron Miksa believes the lift requires a minimal amount of weight from the base weight to enable motion with hand held. noticed no manual control due to strain gage reading low. Let the unit rest a day and then was able to move it manually.
    4. ADC ground is evidently not the same as the separate ground wire run to the DIO.


  3. NBS pulse installed on new IP DID48 accurate to 14 usecs which is the resolution of the signal, ±9V square wave. Currently interrupting on level twice per second, but ignoring low level interrupt.

  4. I think it was a productive trip. As soon as Peter Pietro's Glen-Tek amplifiers are installed, I'll be looking for another opportunity for testing at APO. This trip provided feedback for what is needed for monitoring and diagnostics for telescope motion. Also, on the way home, I visited the Very Large Array (VLA) to make a contact and obtain information.
