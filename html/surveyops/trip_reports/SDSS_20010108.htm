Notes from trip to SDSS at Apache Point January 8-12, 2001.

I traveled to APO on January 8, 2001 and returned on January 12. While at APO I accomplished the following.

  1. Checked out the wiring for the Secondary Mirror Support System (SMSS) servo control chassis.
  2. Troubleshot the prototype Piezo Servo Controller circuit card and made the appropriate modifications to make it work.
  3. Installed, calibrated and tested the Piezo Servo Controller with the piezo actuators on the SMSS as an individual unit. I did not have an opportunity to test the other components of the servo controller chassis (e.g. the DAC interface card).
  4. Troubleshot SMSS actuator readout problem and found that the Hiedenhiem encoders had been replaced on the wrong axes after some previous maintenance work. I relabeled the cables and reconnected the encoder cables to the proper inputs on the servo controller chassis.
  5. Investigated details associated with the future modifications of the DC power distribution on the telescope.
  6. Assisted in the investigation of Altitude positioning troubles. These problems were eventually corrected by installing a new MEI servo controller board.
