Sponsors

The Alfred P. Sloan Foundation, a philanthropic non-profit institution, was established by Alfred P. Sloan, Jr., in 1934. The Foundation is supporting the Sloan Digital Sky Survey with grants for the development, construction and commissioning phases of the project.

The National Aeronautics and Space Administration has supported a portion of SDSS construction and commissioning, with the aim of providing data essential to operate and reduce data from multiple NASA space missions.

The National Science Foundation, a federal agency that supports fundamental research in all fields of science and engineering, funded the planning of the Sky Survey and has also contributed toward construction of the telescopes and instruments used in the survey. NSF supports such projects that provide broad access to data for the greater scientific community.

Through its funding of Fermilab, a Department of Energy National Laboratory, DOE provides major ongoing support for the Sky Survey. This support from the Department of Energy, a longtime advocate of high-energy physics research, reflects the increasing overlap between particle physics and cosmology.

The Japanese Monbukagakusho (The Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science, and Technology of Japan) aids the SDSS through special promotion grants to the Japan Participation Group for the Research Center for the Early Universe (RESCEU).

The Max Planck Society supports the SDSS through operating and supplemental grants to its participating institutes, the Max-Planck-Institute for Astronomy in Heidelberg and the Max-Planck-Institute for Astrophysics in Garching.
