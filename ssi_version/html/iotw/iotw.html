Image of the Week:Face on Spiral Galaxy NGC60

Located in the constellation of Pisces, at a red shift of 0.039, the spiral Galaxy NGC60 reveals large extended spiral arms extending much further out than as expected on the first glance. The low surface brightness of the spiral arms makes it difficult to detect them against the light of the sky background. In order to reveal this structures six exposures in a individual color band of the SDSS-2.5m telescope were added and thereby reducing the noise in the background.
The striking kink located roughly 17 kpc away from the center of the galaxy is most probably a star burst triggered from gravitational interaction with a neighboring galaxy. With a scale length of the disk of approximately 14 kpc, the galaxy is nearly three times the spatial size as the Milky way.
Also visible in the image are two asteroids in rainbow like colors moving close to the ecliptic.
